## About Me

I am Yunpeng Li, an Associate Professor at the Institute of Information Engineering, Chinese Academy of Sciences, where I was selected for an institute talent program. I hold a Ph.D. from the same institute. My work sits at the intersection of large language models, AI security, and trustworthy intelligent systems, with a long-term focus on building models and agentic systems that are reliable, secure, and useful in real operational environments.

My research interests include trustworthy and safe large language models, LLM-based agents, intelligent agent hubs and orchestration, AI-enabled cybersecurity, and risk-aware decision support systems. I am particularly interested in how large models can be made more robust against misuse, backdoors, and unreliable behaviors, and how they can be integrated into practical workflows for vulnerability discovery, security analysis, cross-lingual information access, and cyber threat intelligence.

I have led or contributed to multiple national-level and provincial research projects, including programs related to key infrastructure, the National Key Research and Development Program of China, major national science and technology projects, and joint key funding from the National Natural Science Foundation of China. My research has resulted in more than 40 publications in leading international conferences and journals, including AAAI, NeurIPS, SIGIR, ACL, and related venues. Beyond publications, my technical work has been adopted by national ministries and government agencies and used in cybersecurity assurance for major national events, including the Winter Olympics, the National Two Sessions, and the September 3rd military parade. I also led the development of a regulatory business system deployed at the national level, where it has become a core operational platform for analysis, risk perception, and early-warning support.

## Research Interests

- **Large Language Models:** reasoning control, LLM safety, backdoor defense, multilingual generation and retrieval
- **Network Security:** malware classification, clean-label attacks, robust and trustworthy learning systems
- **Natural Language Processing:** dialogue systems, controllable generation, machine translation, cross-lingual information retrieval
